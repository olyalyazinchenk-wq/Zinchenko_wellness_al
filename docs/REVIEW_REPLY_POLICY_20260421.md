# Review Reply Policy

Date: 2026-04-21
Owner: Olga / Wellness Bot
Purpose: define how the bot and operator reply to every client review in a way that is warm, objective, and brand-consistent.

## Core Decision

Every client review should receive a reply.

But the reply should not behave like:

- flattery,
- emotional mirroring,
- automatic appeasement,
- defensive argument.

The correct tone is:

- warm,
- respectful,
- emotionally steady,
- honest,
- premium,
- objective.

## What "With Soul But Without Fawning" Means

The product should sound human and attentive.

At the same time, it should not:

- agree with everything automatically,
- copy the client's irritation,
- become sugary when praised,
- collapse into apology theater when criticized.

The reply should show two things at once:

1. the client was heard,
2. the brand still has a spine.

## Mandatory Rules

### 1. Reply to every review

Silence after a review weakens trust.

This applies to:

- positive reviews,
- mixed reviews,
- critical reviews,
- short dry reviews.

### 2. Positive review != exaggerated gratitude

If the review is positive:

- thank the client,
- name the value that mattered,
- keep the reply short and grounded.

Do not write:

- "you are the best client",
- "your words mean everything",
- "we are endlessly grateful".

### 3. Mixed review = strongest product signal

If the review is mixed:

- thank for honesty,
- separate what worked from what did not,
- acknowledge the weak point calmly,
- do not justify the weakness.

### 4. Critical review should not trigger submission

If the review is critical:

- reply calmly,
- do not fight,
- do not guilt the client,
- do not flatter to neutralize tension,
- invite one precise clarification if useful.

### 5. Facts may be corrected

If the client writes something factually inaccurate, the reply may gently correct it.

Example:

- if the client says the service "set a diagnosis",
- the reply may calmly state that the product does not diagnose and is built as nutrition navigation.

This is not conflict.

This is brand clarity.

### 6. Medical boundary must remain visible

If the review mentions:

- diagnosis,
- treatment,
- prescription,
- therapy,

the reply should remind:

`Мы не ставим диагнозы и не назначаем лечение. Задача разбора — помочь увидеть возможные риски и следующий шаг.`

## Tone Rules

The answer should feel:

- mature,
- composed,
- high-touch,
- thoughtful.

The answer should not feel:

- needy,
- defensive,
- overly cute,
- passive-aggressive,
- submissive.

## Recommended Structure

### Positive review

1. thank for the feedback,
2. reflect the value that landed,
3. close calmly.

### Mixed review

1. thank for honesty,
2. name the useful criticism,
3. confirm that it is being taken into product improvement.

### Critical review

1. thank for directness,
2. state that the mismatch matters,
3. if useful, ask what specific area felt weakest,
4. add factual clarification if needed.

## Operational Principle

The bot may generate the first draft of a reply.

But for early pilot cases:

- sensitive replies,
- harsh criticism,
- legally blurry cases,
- medically loaded complaints inside reviews,

should still be reviewed by a human before sending.
