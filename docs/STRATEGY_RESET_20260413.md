# Strategy Reset

Date: 2026-04-13
Decision owner: Chief Orchestrator
Status: Active

## Executive Decision

The strategy does need refinement.

This is not a hard pivot.
This is a strategic sharpening.

We keep:

- Telegram-first
- Russian-contour preference
- human-in-the-loop review
- premium positioning
- `Premium Wellness Dossier` as the flagship paid artifact

We change:

- `Premium Wellness Dossier` is no longer treated as the first market wedge
- the first wedge becomes **clarity and navigation in one chat thread**
- the product is managed as a **premium service operating system**, not as a platform

## Why The Reset Is Needed

The strategy docs are stronger than the current product reality.

Right now:

- bot stability is improving
- ops control exists
- positioning exists
- but real conversational value is still weaker than the premium promise
- review workflow is not yet fully operationalized
- the exact first paid trigger is not pinned tightly enough

This means the main strategic risk is not lack of ambition.
It is false confidence.

## New Strategic Frame

### What the user is really buying

The user is not primarily buying a dossier.

The user is buying:

- fast clarity
- reduced anxiety
- a trustworthy next step
- the feeling of being personally guided

### Product structure

The product now has two layers:

1. **Entry wedge**

`Wellness Clarity in Telegram`

Promise:

- understand what may be going on
- get a concrete next step
- stay in one calm, guided conversation

2. **Core paid service**

`Premium Wellness Dossier`

Promise:

- structured case synthesis
- reviewed expert output
- prioritized actions and follow-up path

The dossier remains central for monetization.
It is no longer the main acquisition promise.

## ICP Lock

The only ICP for now:

- women 28-45
- fatigue, digestive complaints, unclear symptom clusters
- prior frustration with "everything is normal"

Everything else becomes later.

## What Must Stop

- speaking as if we already have a platform
- designing for three audiences at once
- treating the dossier as the only visible source of value
- broad “premium wellness” messaging without a sharp buying trigger
- building extra complexity before proving throughput and conversion

## What Must Be True

The first successful version of this product must prove all of the following:

1. A cold or warm user can quickly get useful clarity in Telegram.
2. The conversation feels personal and premium, not generic.
3. The user clearly understands why a paid dossier is worth buying now.
4. Olga can review and deliver cases fast enough to sustain the promise.
5. Compliance, consent, storage, and escalation are part of the product design itself.

## Compliance As Product Constraint

Compliance is not a footer concern.
It is part of the strategy.

This affects:

- channel choice
- data retention
- consent UX
- escalation wording
- human review requirement
- runtime/provider choices

If compliance becomes painful, the offer and workflow must adapt.
We do not force the product through a broken legal or trust surface.

## New North Star

`From confusion to a trustworthy next step in one Telegram thread.`

## New Success Hierarchy

Priority order:

1. useful first interaction
2. intake completion
3. reviewed case throughput
4. paid dossier conversion
5. follow-up retention

Not the other way around.

## Metrics That Matter Now

- first useful reply rate
- generic reply rate
- intake completion rate
- review turnaround time
- time to dossier delivery
- paid conversion to dossier
- repeat follow-up conversion

## Strategic Verdict

Verdict: `sharpen`

The direction is right.
The promise, sequencing, and operating assumptions needed correction.

This is now officially a premium clarity-and-navigation service with a dossier as the core monetized artifact.
